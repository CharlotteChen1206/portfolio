Neon Vice – Illustrated Covers on Substance Addiction

Digital illustration (Procreate), Moodboarding & concept developmen, Adobe Illustrator, Visual storytelling & emotional design

Introduction

Neon Vice is a series of three digital cover illustrations exploring the themes of substance addiction—specifically cigarettes, alcohol, and drugs. Created using Procreate and Adobe Illustrator, the goal was to deliver an emotionally impactful and visually bold set of artworks that raise awareness through stylized design. The process began with developing a personal signature, building a mood board, and crafting a unique visual identity. I used a high-contrast color palette and neon-style handwritten titles to evoke intensity and draw immediate attention. Each piece captures the allure and danger of addiction in a surreal, almost glowing aesthetic to leave a lasting impression.

Research & Data

I explored visual trends in editorial illustrations, analyzed poster design techniques that use contrast and glow effects, and studied symbolic color usage related to addiction and nightlife. The neon text style was inspired by signs often associated with bars or clubs—spaces where such substances are prevalent.
